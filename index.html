The HSR Project & Benefits

Corridor & Goal

The Empire Corridor (NYC to Albany/Buffalo). Goal: Use modern electric trains and infrastructure upgrades to achieve competitive, reliable travel times.

Economic Impact

HSR generates approximately four dollars in broader economic benefits for every $1 invested, linking key metropolitan areas.

Environmental Mandate

HSR produces 83% fewer greenhouse gas emissions than driving. Compliance with strict standards makes it a critical tool for climate goals.

Implementation Roadmap

The project is structured in two main phases to expedite construction in the highest-priority section (NYP to Albany).

Phase 1: NYP to Albany

Builds the high-priority section, including new dedicated right-of-ways to eliminate sharp turns, alongside the initial order of new electric trains.

Phase 2: Albany to Buffalo

Begins while Phase 1 is in testing. This phase requires extensive track upgrades and further train set orders to match projected ridership growth.

Testing & Compliance

Rigorous non-passenger operation testing in both phases ensures safety and 100% adherence to all federal regulations (RLA, Buy America, NEPA/CEQA).

Mandatory Commitments

HRP is committed to maximizing American job creation, equity, and inclusion:

  • Labor Guarantee: The operating entity will guarantee "rail carrier" status" compliance, ensuring workers receive traditional railroad employee benefits (Railway Labor Act, Railroad Retirement Act).
  • Sourcing: Compliance with "Buy America" requirements for steel, iron, and manufactured goods to bolster the domestic manufacturing base.
  • Workforce Equity: Implementation of a robust Business Workforce Opportunity Program (BWOP) and Project Labor Agreement (PLA) for local job creation and equity.
